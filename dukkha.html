Dukkha & Tetris

A major root of suffering (dukkha) in life is impermanence—everything constantly shifting beneath our feet. When the stories and assumptions we rely on start to crumble, our mental scaffolding can collapse, leaving us anxious, overwhelmed, or even depressed.

Interestingly, there’s growing evidence that a simple, old-school video game can help steady the mind in those moments:

  • This video game might actually be good for your mental health (National Geographic)
  • How Tetris helps my stress and anxiety melt away (Wired)

Meditation works wonders for many people, but it’s not for everyone. Sometimes stacking colorful blocks as they fall—relentlessly, predictably, one by one—is exactly the kind of focused, absorbing distraction the mind needs to regroup.
