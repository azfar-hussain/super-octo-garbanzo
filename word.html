As Per Word...

The true extent of your agency—your real ability to shape your life and the world around you—depends far less on personal qualities alone and much more on the social currencies you command: status, fame, power, and wealth.

You can be extraordinarily creative, influential, brilliant, hardworking, resilient, and fiercely independent. Yet these traits remain largely potential—subjective and unproven—until they are converted into tangible social currency. That conversion only happens when you step into the arena and engage with the world.

A word of caution: chasing status, fame, power, or wealth purely for their own sake is dangerous. It breeds short-term thinking and fragile foundations, because all social currencies are fleeting by nature.

So what truly lasts? What is worth pursuing?

Character growth and personal development.
The deliberate choice to seek out challenges that forge you.

These are the only things that endure.
