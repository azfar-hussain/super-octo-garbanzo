Healthy Coping Mechanisms

There is universal agreement that learning to cope effectively — especially under stress — is essential. To evaluate and compare different coping strategies, we can assess them across the following key dimensions:

  1. Time horizon: Short-term relief vs. long-term benefits
  2. Ease of implementation: Simple and accessible vs. complex or overwhelming
  3. Sustainability: Low-cost and maintainable vs. expensive or resource-intensive
  4. Adaptiveness: Promotes growth and learning (adaptive) vs. harmful or regressive (maladaptive)
  5. Effectiveness: Actually reduces stress and solves problems vs. ineffective or counterproductive
